Elyon Chain Documentation

Everything validators and delegators need to work with Elyon Chain — an EVM Layer-1 running patched Nethermind with APOS (Authorized Proof of Stake) consensus. Run a validator. Build a pool. Share the fees.

Tip: click “Save as PDF” (top right) to download any section as a PDF for offline reading or sharing with your team.

Add Elyon Chain to your wallet

Elyon is EVM-compatible, so MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Rabby, etc. all work. Add the network once:

  • Network name: Elyon Chain
  • RPC URL: https://admintestnet.elyon.network/rpc
  • Chain ID: 89178316
  • Currency symbol: ELN
  • Block explorer: https://explorer.elyon.network
Wallets reject plain http:// RPC URLs — always use the https://…/rpc endpoint above. If a network was added earlier with a bad URL, remove it and re-add it.

Become a Validator

Validators are the backbone of Elyon Chain: each one runs their own node, stakes into the APOS registry, and — once admin-approved — seals blocks and earns a share of the network’s transaction fees.

1. Run your own node

docker run -d --name elyonchain --restart unless-stopped \
  -p 3000:3000 -p 30303:30303 -p 8545:8545 \
  -v aposunp-data:/data \
  -e NODE_ROLE=operator \
  -e ADMIN_URL=https://admintestnet.elyon.network \
  elyonchain/node:latest

The node auto-syncs from the admin bootstrap URL. Open http://<your-server>:3000/manager — the node panel — to manage it.

2. Apply as a validator — through a staking package

From the node panel, pick one of the admin's staking packages and click Apply as Validator. Applying stakes at least the larger of the network minimum and the package minimum into the APOS registry from your validator wallet, selects that package for you and locks the stake for the package term. Your status shows as PENDING.

One package at a time. You can top up your selected package any time (your whole stake re-locks for the same term), but switching to a different package is only possible once the current term has ended. There is no early exit from a package term.

3. Wait for admin approval

The admin reviews and approves your application. On approval your status flips to ACTIVE and you receive a unique public APOS code — the handle delegators use to fund your pool. Share it anywhere.

4. Climb the tier curve — with your POOL

Your tier — your share of the network’s transaction fees — is set by the admin’s tier curve and is read from one number: your POOL = your own package-locked stake plus every delegator deposit. Delegations count toward your tier directly — attracting delegators is tier progress in itself. Bigger pool, higher tier, bigger fee cut. The tier curve governs the fee share only; APR comes exclusively from the staking package you stake into.

5. Earn daily profit — claim any time

Your package APR accrues continuously (daily) on your own staked balance. You don't wait for the term to end: hit Claim daily profit in the panel whenever you like and the accrued amount is paid out from the network reward pool. Your principal stays locked until the package term ends.

6. Stay above the minimum — auto-removal & auto-recovery

Your pool (own stake + delegations) must stay at or above the network's minimum validator stake. If it ever drops below — for example after withdrawing your stake when the lock elapses — the registry automatically moves you to BELOW_MIN: your gas-fee share is pulled and you earn nothing while below the minimum. Nothing is lost, though: the moment the pool is back at the minimum — by topping up your package or by receiving new delegations (delegating to a below-minimum validator is allowed exactly so its pool can be refilled) — you are automatically restored to ACTIVE and your tier is re-applied. No admin action needed in either direction.

Validator status values: NONE → PENDING → ACTIVE (or REJECTED / SUSPENDED, set by the admin, and BELOW_MIN, set automatically when the pool drops under the minimum). Only ACTIVE validators appear on the public site and the Validator Leaderboard.

The pool — one number drives everything

Every validator has one pool in the registry, made of two parts:

  • Your own stake — ELN locked through the admin’s staking packages (your apply stake plus top-ups). It earns the package APR, accrued daily and claimable any time; the principal unlocks when the package term elapses. One package at a time — switching requires the term to end.
  • Delegator deposits — made through your published packages (see two-tier packages below) from your public validator page. Each deposit is its own position that earns the package's rate and counts toward your tier immediately. Delegator money is never yours to move: delegators withdraw their positions themselves, or you can release a position back to them.
In short: one pool — your stake + delegations — drives the tier. Your daily profit comes from your package APR; your delegators' daily profit comes from the package terms you market to them (≤ your package APR); your fee share comes from the tier curve.

Two-tier packages — the marketing model

Elyon's staking economics work on two tiers:

  • Tier 1 — admin → validator. The chain admin publishes the staking packages (lock term, minimum, APR) and approves validators who stake through one. The package APR is the validator's wholesale rate — the most it can earn on its own stake.
  • Tier 2 — validator → delegators. Each approved validator becomes a marketer of its own pool: it publishes its own delegation packages on its public page (/validator.html?code=…) — each with a name, a monthly rate, a lock term, a minimum deposit per position (no maximum), and an early-exit penalty (max 20%).

The rules that keep it honest

  • The cap: a tier-2 package's rate can never exceed the validator's tier-1 package APR — a validator can't promise delegators more than its own package earns. (Monthly rate cap = package APR ÷ 12.)
  • One package per delegator: a delegator can hold open positions in only one package of one validator at a time — top-ups of that same package are always allowed, but joining a different package (or another validator) requires withdrawing the current package fully first.
  • Snapshotted forever: when a delegator joins a package, its rate, lock term and penalty are written into the position — grandfathered. Editing or disabling the package later never touches existing positions.
  • Per-package penalty: withdrawing a position before its lock term ends deducts the package's penalty (e.g. 5%) from the withdrawn principal and credits it to the validator. After the lock ends, withdrawals are penalty-free. A validator-initiated release is always penalty-free.
  • Validator approval: joining goes through the validator's desk — the delegator picks a package, sends a request, and the deposit form unlocks once the validator accepts.
  • Profile: each validator publishes a public profile (company, contact, address, website, about) rendered on its page — so delegators know who they are dealing with.
Think of it like telecoms: the network operator (admin) sets wholesale terms; resellers (validators) package them into retail plans. The chain enforces that no retail plan ever pays more than the wholesale rate.

Delegators

No hardware, no node — just back a validator you trust:

  1. Get ELN (testnet: use the Faucet).
  2. Open a validator's public page (every validator card on the home page and the delegator portal links to it) and compare its packages: monthly rate, lock term, minimum deposit and early-exit penalty — each package has a minimum deposit you must meet to join.
  3. Join a package: connect your wallet, send the validator a join request and chat it out. Once the validator accepts, the deposit form unlocks for that package.
  4. Deposit any amount at or above the package minimum (there is no maximum) — each deposit creates a new position with the package's rate, lock and penalty locked in at deposit, forever (later package changes never touch it). You can hold multiple positions, but only within one package of one validator at a time — to join a different validator or package, withdraw your current package fully first. Deposits count toward the validator's tier directly.
  5. Profit accrues daily on every position at its grandfathered rate — watch it tick live under “My positions”.
  6. Withdraw yourself, any time — principal (full or partial) plus accrued profit goes straight back to your wallet. ⚠ Early withdrawal: exiting before the package's lock term ends pays that package's early-exit penalty on the withdrawn principal (kept by the validator). The validator can also release a position back to you — releases carry no penalty.
  7. Validators may additionally send manual gifts at their discretion — shown as “total received” in the portal.
Your terms are fixed the moment you deposit. Compare validators by their package terms (rate, lock, penalty — all capped by their package APR), profile, pool size, position count and leaderboard rank — all public signals.

Validator panel

Your node’s /manager page is mission control for your validator business:

  • Pool overview — your pool (one number, drives the tier) broken into your own stake and delegator deposits, current tier (fee share %) and your selected package’s APR.
  • Daily profit — the package APR accrued so far on your stake, with Claim daily profit to cash it out any time.
  • My Packages — create and manage the tier-2 packages you market to delegators: name, lock days, monthly rate (capped at package APR ÷ 12), minimum deposit and early-exit penalty. Enable/disable any time — existing positions keep their snapshotted terms.
  • My Page — your public profile (company, contact, address, website, about) plus a link to your validator page (/validator.html?code=YOURCODE) — share it everywhere.
  • Delegation requests — delegators ask to join one of your packages (the chosen package is shown on the request); chat, then accept or decline.
  • Positions table — every delegation position (delegator, label, package, amount, rate, accrued profit); name delegators, release principal back (no penalty, profit paid too) or send manual gifts.
  • Package — top up your selected tier-1 package (re-locks the same term) or switch packages after the term ends.
  • Fee earnings — your accumulated share of the transaction fees collected by the network, plus any early-exit penalties from your delegators.

Gas & fees — standard EIP-1559

Elyon Chain uses standard Ethereum EIP-1559 gas pricing — no custom fee market:

  • Base fee — set per block by protocol and burned.
  • Priority fee (tip) — collected with each transaction.
  • Validator fee share — your tier’s fee-share % applies to the collected fees, credited to approved validators.

Any EVM wallet or tooling that speaks EIP-1559 (MetaMask, ethers.js, viem, Foundry…) works unchanged.

ElyonID for product integrations

ElyonID is your wallet address on Elyon Chain — the identifier other products use to credit, pay, or attribute you. To integrate:

  • Fetch the user's ElyonID by requesting their connected address via EIP-1193 (eth_requestAccounts) on chain ID 89178316.
  • Verify ownership by asking the user to sign a nonce (personal_sign) and recovering the address.
  • Resolve on-chain roles (node / contract owner / token owner) via the APOS registry read endpoints exposed by the dashboard (/api/apos/*).
Need deeper integration help (registry ABIs, fee-share webhooks)? Use Report Issue with category “Other” and describe your use case — the team will follow up.
